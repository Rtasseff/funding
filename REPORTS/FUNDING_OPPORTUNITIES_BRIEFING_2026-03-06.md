### 8. HORIZON-HLTH-2027-02-TOOL-01 — Predictive Biomarkers Using AI

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Funder** | European Commission / Horizon Europe Cluster 1 Health |
| **What it funds** | AI-driven predictive biomarkers for disease progression and treatment response, using multimodal data (omics, imaging, clinical, sensor) |
| **Amount** | €6-8M per project (~6 projects funded) |
| **Deadline** | Stage 1: 2027-04-13 / Stage 2: 2027-09-22 (~403 days to Stage 1) |
| **Eligibility** | EU-wide consortium. CIC biomaGUNE eligible as partner. |
| **Status** | Pre-announcement (2026-2027 Work Programme published; call opens Feb 2027) |

**Why this matters:** This is the single strongest EU opportunity for the full program. Three modules score 3/3:
- **MOD-AI-PIPELINES:** Foundation-model-based imaging biomarker extraction with uncertainty quantification
- **MOD-THEORY-AI:** Theory-infused AI for predictive modeling of disease progression
- **MOD-MULTIMODAL-BIOMARKERS:** Multimodal biomarker discovery — the core call scope

CIC biomaGUNE's theory-infused AI framework is a genuine differentiator in this space. Most consortia will propose standard deep learning; physics-constrained latent dynamics models are novel.

**What to prepare (start now, ramp up Oct 2026):**
- Use AEI GPE2026 to fund preparation costs
- Identify consortium partners: clinical centers with NCD imaging cohorts, omics partners, 1-2 other AI/modeling groups
- Develop the translational bridge: preclinical imaging → clinical imaging cohorts (need named clinical partners)
- Prepare a 1-page concept for consortium recruitment
- Build preliminary results on real preclinical data by end of 2026

**Risk:** Requires a multi-partner consortium with clinical data access. Two-stage evaluation is resource-intensive. Preclinical-only positioning is insufficient — need translational partners. 13 months to Stage 1 gives time but consortium assembly must start early.
