### AEI Proyectos de Generación de Conocimiento (PID2026)
- **Funder:** Agencia Estatal de Investigación (AEI)
- **Source:** AEI Convocatorias
- **Deadline:** 2026-11-03 to 2026-11-24 (~242d remaining)
- **Status:** pre-announcement (AEI calendar published; call not yet open)
- **URL:** https://www.aei.gob.es/convocatorias/buscador-convocatorias
- **Summary:** The main Spanish competitive research call, funding 3-4 year knowledge-generation projects across all disciplines. Typically €200M+ total budget. Open to all public research organizations (OPIs, CICs, universities). Theory-infused AI fits naturally as fundamental research in computational biology, applied mathematics, or physics-informed ML.
- **Track:** B
- **Module fit:**
  - MOD-FAIR-TRAIN: 1 — FAIR practices can be a minor WP but not the focus of a knowledge-generation project
  - MOD-IMG-SERVERS: 0 — Infrastructure is not funded under this call
  - MOD-AI-PIPELINES: 2 — AI/ML methodology development for imaging is fundable but the call favors fundamental research over service delivery
  - MOD-THEORY-AI: 3 — Strong natural fit. Theory-infused AI (physics-informed ML, latent ODE/PDE, manifold-constrained dynamics) is core fundamental research. This is the ideal vehicle for the formulation work.
  - MOD-MULTIMODAL-BIOMARKERS: 2 — Multimodal computational biology with a PI anchor and defined dataset is fundable
- **Confidence:** HIGH — CIC biomaGUNE is directly eligible. Theory-infused AI is genuine fundamental research that maps to AEI's knowledge-generation scope. November deadline gives 8 months to prepare. Can request 1-2 PIs and a research team. Typical grant size €100K-300K.
- **Red flags:** Highly competitive. Budget per project is modest (not program-scale). Need to define a focused project within MOD-THEORY-AI rather than the full program. Requires preliminary results or formulation work as credibility evidence.

### HORIZON-HLTH-2027-02-TOOL-01 — Predictive Biomarkers Using AI (two-stage)
- **Funder:** European Commission / Horizon Europe Cluster 1 Health
- **Source:** EU Funding & Tenders Portal
- **Deadline:** Stage 1: 2027-04-13, Stage 2: 2027-09-22 (~403d to Stage 1)
- **Status:** pre-announcement (2026-2027 Work Programme published; call opens Feb 2027)
- **URL:** https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/portal/screen/opportunities/calls-for-proposals
- **Summary:** Development of predictive biomarkers of disease progression and treatment response using AI methodologies for chronic non-communicable diseases. Explicitly calls for multimodal data (omics, imaging, clinical, sensor-derived) and AI-driven biomarker discovery/validation. Two-stage evaluation.
- **Track:** B
- **Module fit:**
  - MOD-FAIR-TRAIN: 1 — FAIR data as a WP requirement, not the core scope
  - MOD-IMG-SERVERS: 2 — Imaging data infrastructure as an enabling WP within a consortium
  - MOD-AI-PIPELINES: 3 — Strong natural fit. Foundation-model-based pipelines for imaging biomarker extraction, validated with domain-shift testing and uncertainty quantification — exactly what this call asks for.
  - MOD-THEORY-AI: 3 — Strong natural fit. Theory-infused AI for predictive modeling of disease progression, constrained latent dynamics for trajectory forecasting — this is the differentiator.
  - MOD-MULTIMODAL-BIOMARKERS: 3 — Direct fit. Multimodal biomarker discovery with imaging is the core scope.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — Three modules at 3/3. This is the single best EU call for the full program. CIC biomaGUNE's theory-infused AI framework applied to preclinical imaging biomarkers is a strong differentiator. 2027 deadline gives 13 months to build consortium and prepare Stage 1.
- **Red flags:** 2027 call (not 2026). Requires clinical/translational consortium partners with patient cohorts. Two-stage evaluation is resource-intensive. CIC biomaGUNE would need named clinical partners and access to NCD imaging cohorts. Preclinical-only positioning may be insufficient — need translational bridge.
