## One-sentence positioning

An institute-wide training and adoption program that makes FAIR practice operational: repeatable DMPs, minimum metadata habits, and publication-linked data packaging that increases internal reuse and AI readiness.

## Current state

- **Base DMP template + example DMP + FAQ seed list** delivered (from Workshop 2 kit).
- **DMP-to-Group protocol v0.1** underway — lightweight SOP steps for group-level adoption.
- **Officialization package** in progress: Master RDM SOP + DMP SOP + OA archiving SOP + policy edit pipeline.
- OA archiving workflow operational with packaging templates.
- RDM workshops delivered with follow-up office hours model.
- Adoption is growing but uneven; systematic onboarding + metrics needed to scale.

## Next-stage objective (12-24 months)

1. Standardize the curriculum (onboarding + annual refresh + role-based modules).
2. Convert policies/templates into micro-lessons + checklists.
3. Implement minimal metrics collection (adoption + reuse + compliance).
4. Publish a lightweight service model (office hours, intake, escalation, RACI).

## Longer-term trajectory (3-5 years)

- Move from "minimum compliance" to domain-aware FAIR where proportional.
- Add automation (DMP helpers, metadata validation, publication linkage).

## Why it matters

- Improves compliance posture and reduces last-minute administrative load.
- Makes internal reuse plausible (people can find and interpret datasets).
- Reduces downstream friction for imaging servers and AI pipelines.

## Typical proposal configuration

- **Track A:** institutional enablement + adoption metrics
- **Track B:** embedded WP in PI-led projects so adoption is real

## Risks / mitigations

- Scope creep into "the service writes DMPs": mitigate via explicit boundary.
- Low adoption: mitigate via lighthouse pilots and PI anchors.
